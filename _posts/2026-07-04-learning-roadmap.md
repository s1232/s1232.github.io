# Vacation time

## Getting up early
It is July 4th, the second day of our trip to Peroj, Croatia. We have rented a nice house together with some friends. This morning I got 05:30AM after going to bed early. It is mentally and physically impossible to sleep more than 7-7.5 hours and getting up earlier than everone else offers some quite time to think and reflect.

10 months ago I created [my very first blog post](https://s1232.github.io/2025/09/28/soft-hyphen-in-json-text.html), in an attempt to structure my own learning. That post, is a typical example of messing around with some problem and instead of leaving the problem the second it has been resolved, I wanted to force myself to writing a cohesive summary. The expectation is of course that the writing process would be valuable in itself. I have no aspirations of the content being valuable to others. The blog serves as a destination for thinkering and a structure for writing.

It is unbelivable that there I have added ZERO new blog posts the last ten months. Did I learn nothing all this time? Certainly not, but I failed miserably in writing it down. I still believe in the concept and refuse to give up.

## Learning roadmap
It is probably too passive to think of the blog as strictly a learning log. Let's instead build out a high level plan for future learning, some kind of roadmap with a directional impulse. I don't use AI for writing, which should be obvious from my clunky Norwegian-influenced writing style, but I did consult ChatGPT when coming up with this roadmap:

The disussed was around spending time choosing between Rust and Java as well as breaking the habit of consuming and superficially tapping into the learning of others. Since I have already some momentum with Rust I decided against switching to Java now, spending even more time restarting. There are real reasons to approach Java instead, its massive adoption being the prominent one. This will have to come later.

So the starting point is Rust, open source and data engineering or integration software.

1. Become comfortable reading the internals of Apache DataFusion.
2. Learn the Apache Arrow memory model and the Apache Parquet format well enough to explain them to someone else.
3. Make several small documentation, test, or bug-fix contributions to DataFusion.
4. Build a small SQL engine or Parquet analysis tool in Rust to deepen your understanding.

There it is, my learning roadmap over the coming six months, July through December 2026. I will close out this blog post now to get to it.
